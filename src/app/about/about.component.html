About Me

I have had a very different path into software engineering than most other engineers. I majored in Liberal Arts at a college which was founded on the Socratic Method (as opposed to the lecture method) that demanded that I think out of the box and understand the “why” of things. Now when I graduated I applied this to the business world of Strategic Sourcing at a Fortune 30 company, Anthem Inc. I started as a consultant and quickly excelled and in no time they wanted to hire as an employee of their own. However, I wanted more, I needed a bigger challenge. I left Anthem in May and started to pave my path as a Software Engineer. I was lucky enough to land a job as a part of OrangeTech as a part time employee and get my feet wet in the field. In my spare time I became the head coach of my old high school’s football team. Most would say this choice to be a coach was not the most professionally efficient decision, shouldn’t I be spending my time honing my skills as a developer instead of coaching high school boys? Wrong. I quickly realized coaching would be the single biggest asset I bring to any professional team. I had to be a leader, coach team work to young men, be a good example and help teach these kids what it means to work together for a common goal. I am new to the software field, but I am a very quick learner and a harder worker. If given the opportunity I will apply my leadership abilities I gained through coaching and seamlessly work with the team for the common goals of the company. I have learned Node JS, Angular, MySql, html, css, and flutter and I am very eager to apply this knowledge as a part of your team.
